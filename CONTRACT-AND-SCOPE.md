# Contract & Scope — Formalizing the Client Engagement

Use this to formalize the project, get clear sign-off, and feel confident about collecting payment after a long engagement.

---

## 1. Is "Contract Killer" a good contract to use?

**What Contract Killer is:**  
Contract Killer is a **plain-language freelance contract** originally by Andy Clarke (Stuff & Nonsense). It’s widely used by designers and developers. It covers scope, payment, revisions, IP, and termination in readable (non-legalese) language.

**Samuel Sycamore’s version:**  
You mentioned `https://github.com/samuelsycamore/contract-killer` — that repo returns 404, so it may have been moved, renamed, or made private. Sycamore’s version was a **US English / California-oriented** adaptation of the original.

**Alternatives:**

- **Official Contract Killer (paid):** [Stuff & Nonsense – Killer Templates](https://stuffandnonsense.co.uk/projects/killer-templates) — Contract Killer is now **£10.99** (HTML, Markdown, PDF, Word). The **Killer Bundle** (£15.99) includes NDA, Privacy, Proposals, and Contract Killer. Includes 1 year of updates.
- **Free / open:** Search for “Contract Killer” on [Docracy](https://www.docracy.com) or GitHub; you may find community copies or forks. Always compare to the official wording and tailor to your situation.
- **Lawyer:** For a project that’s been running ~1 year and involves real money, having a local attorney review or draft a short contract (or letter of agreement) is a solid option, especially for your state/jurisdiction.

**Verdict:**  
Contract Killer–style contracts are **good as a base**: clear, fair, and familiar in the industry. If you can’t find the Sycamore repo, use the paid Contract Killer or a lawyer-drafted agreement. Whatever you use, **customize** it (names, dates, payment amount, deliverables) and consider one-time legal review.

## 2. What to include in your contract / agreement

At minimum, put in writing:

| Item | Why |
|------|-----|
| **Parties** | Your name/company and client name (Jermaine Lee Physical Therapy PLLC). |
| **Scope of work** | What the project is: website design/development for the PT practice, including pages listed below (or attach a scope doc). |
| **Deliverables** | What they get: e.g. source files, hosted site (if you host), any specific features (e.g. intake form link). |
| **Payment** | Total fee, due date(s) (e.g. 50% on signing, 50% on launch — or one sum due at completion). How to pay (check, ACH, etc.). |
| **What’s already done** | Short list of what’s been completed over the past year (see Scope & To-Do below). This justifies the final payment. |
| **What’s left (if anything)** | Remaining tasks, or “No remaining tasks; final payment is for work completed.” |
| **Revisions / changes** | How many rounds of revisions are included; that additional work is extra (time/money or out of scope). |
| **Ownership / IP** | That they own the site/copy once paid (or your standard IP terms). |
| **Signatures and date** | Both parties sign and date; keep a copy. |

You can use a **short letter of agreement** (1–2 pages) that references a **Scope & To-Do** document (below) instead of a long contract.

## 2b. Solo freelancer — short letter of agreement (template)

Use this as a one-page agreement. Fill in the bracketed parts, attach or reference the Scope & To-Do (Section 3), and both sign. Keep a signed copy.

---

**AGREEMENT FOR WEBSITE DESIGN / DEVELOPMENT**

**Parties:**  
- **Freelancer:** [Your full name], a sole proprietor (“I” / “Freelancer”)  
- **Client:** Jermaine Lee Physical Therapy PLLC (“Client”)

**Effective date:** [Date]

**1. Scope.** I will provide website design and development services for the Client’s physical therapy practice as described in the attached “Scope & To-Do” document. The project includes the pages, content, and features listed under “Delivered (completed)” and any items agreed in writing as remaining work.

**2. Deliverables.** I will deliver the completed website source files [and / or provide access to the hosted site] as agreed. Client is responsible for providing final copy (contact info, testimonials, team details), hosting/domain decisions, and the New Patient Intake form link to be placed on the site.

**3. Payment.** Client will pay me a total of **$[amount]** for the work described in the Scope & To-Do document. Payment is due [e.g. within 14 days of Client sign-off / upon execution of this agreement and sign-off]. Payment method: [e.g. check, Zelle, ACH, Venmo — specify]. Late payment: [e.g. interest at X% per month after 30 days, or “as agreed in writing.”]

**4. Revisions.** [e.g. Two rounds of revisions for content/layout are included. Additional changes or new features will be quoted separately.]

**5. Ownership.** Upon receipt of full payment, Client owns the custom website design and content created for this project. I retain the right to use the work in my portfolio unless Client requests otherwise in writing.

**6. Independent contractor.** I am an independent contractor, not an employee. Client does not withhold taxes or provide benefits.

**7. Entire agreement.** This agreement and the attached Scope & To-Do constitute the full agreement. Changes must be in writing and signed by both parties.

**Governing law:** The laws of [Your state, e.g. New York] apply.

**Signatures:**

Freelancer: _________________________  Date: _________

Client (Jermaine Lee Physical Therapy PLLC): _________________________  Date: _________

## 3. Scope & To-Do — For client sign-off and payment

Use this as the “deliverables list” you and the client agree on. It makes “what’s done” and “what’s left” explicit so you both feel okay closing the project and collecting.

### 3.1 Delivered (completed)

- [ ] **Multi-page website** for Jermaine Lee Physical Therapy PLLC (Home, About Us, Our Clinicians, Our Services, Contact, Appointment).
- [ ] **Content** tailored to the practice (e.g. Dr. Lee’s story, underserved communities, five boroughs, sports rehab).
- [ ] **Responsive layout** for desktop and mobile.
- [ ] **Request Appointment** section on the home page.
- [ ] **Contact** page and contact info in header/footer.
- [ ] **New Patient Intake** call-to-action on the site (link to Google Form — client adds their form URL).
- [ ] **Placeholder areas** for testimonials, team, and footer contact details (client to supply final copy).

### 3.2 Client to provide (your to-do list for them)

- [ ] **Final contact information:** real address, phone, email for header and all footers (replace placeholder “New York, New York”, “+555-555-5555”, “support@gmail.com”, etc.).
- [ ] **Google Form for New Patient Intake:** create the form, then send you the link so you can set `href` on “Complete New Patient Intake Form” (or embed it if you add that).
- [ ] **Testimonials:** real quotes and permission to use (replace “John Smith” / Lorem ipsum on the site).
- [ ] **Team/clinicians:** confirm names, titles, photos, and social links (replace placeholders like “John Smith”, “Office Manager”).
- [ ] **Hosting and domain:** where the site will live; any login or access you need for launch or handoff.

### 3.3 Optional / future (out of current scope)

- [ ] Connect “Request Appointment” or Contact form to a backend (e.g. Formspree, or their EMR) — or rely on Google Form for intake.
- [ ] FAQ page, blog, or online scheduling link (see CLIENT-VALUE-ADDS.md).
- [ ] Ongoing maintenance or updates (separate agreement if needed).

### 3.4 Sign-off for final payment

- [ ] Client has reviewed the live (or staged) site and approved design and content.
- [ ] Client has received all deliverables (e.g. source code, access to host, or deployed site).
- [ ] Final payment of $________ is due within ________ days of sign-off (fill in your terms).

**Client signature:** _________________________ **Date:** _________  
**Your signature:** _________________________ **Date:** _________

## 4. Suggested next steps

1. **Choose a contract:** Get Contract Killer (paid) or a lawyer-drafted agreement; fill in names, dates, and payment.
2. **Send the client:** (a) Contract or letter of agreement, (b) this CONTRACT-AND-SCOPE.md (or a PDF of the Scope & To-Do section only).
3. **Align on “done”:** Agree that the delivered list (3.1) is complete and that remaining work is either (a) client tasks (3.2) or (b) optional/future (3.3).
4. **Get sign-off:** Client checks the boxes in 3.1 (and 3.2 if you want), signs the sign-off (3.4), and you send invoice for final payment.
5. **After payment:** Hand off any files/logins, and optionally offer a separate maintenance or “value-add” scope (from CLIENT-VALUE-ADDS.md) for future work.

This gives you a clear paper trail, a fair contract, and a concrete to-do list so both of you know what’s done and what’s left — and you can collect with confidence.
